Welcome to Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of power outlets are on the island?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

What are the restrictions on alcohol?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18.

Is English widely spoken?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

What medical facilities are available?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist visitors.

Is Taniti safe for tourists?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Are there national holidays I should be aware of?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency is used on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
